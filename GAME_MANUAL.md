# ❄️ SBM: Snowballmatch Manual

## 📜 Game Concept
Matches are played in rounds between two teams. Each round lasts for a **predefined amount of time**.

* **Progression:** The team that wins a round moves on to the next stage. The losing team is **completely eliminated** from the game.
* **The Split:** After a victory, the winning team is split to form **two new opposing teams**.
* **The Final:** This process repeats until only a **1v1 or 1v2** constellation remains for the **Final Showdown**.


## 🛡️ Match Preparation
At the start of each round, every player begins with **two hearts** and **2 snowballs**. 
Teams are granted a short preparation period to spread out on their respective sides of the field before the battle begins.

## ⚔️ Battle Mechanics

### Combat & Health
* **Damage:** Hitting an opponent with a snowball causes them to permanently lose **one heart** for the duration of the round.
* **Elimination:** If you lose all your hearts, you are out for the rest of the round. You will **rejoin the game** in the next stage if your team wins the round.
* **Strategy:** Use the obstacles across the map to take cover and prepare your next attack.

### Ammunition & Bonuses
* **Ammunition Logic:** When you hit an enemy, your snowball does **not** drop at the player's position. Instead, it is returned to a **dropper** located on your own team's side.
* **Kill Bonus:** If your hit eliminates a player, you are rewarded with **one bonus snowball**.

### Respawning
If you are hit but still have hearts remaining, you will respawn at one of **three designated respawn points** on your team’s side.
* **Indicator:** The active respawn point is marked by a **lit Redstone Lamp**.

---

## 🏆 Victory Conditions
A team can claim victory in one of three ways:

* **A. Annihilation:** Eliminate all players on the opposing team.
* **B. Survival:** Have the **highest total of hearts** when the game timer runs out.
* **C. Objective:** Hit the **"Doom Bell"** located at the back of the enemy's side.

---

## ⚡ Tie-Breaker (Overtime)
Should a round end with both teams having the same total of hearts, a Tie-Breaker begins on a special platform.

1. **Equipment:** Everyone is given a stick with the **Knockback II** enchantment.
2. **Health:** Your health on the platform depends on your team's remaining hearts:

| Hearts at Round End | Tie-Breaker Hearts (Rounded Up) |
| :--- | :--- |
| 1–3 Hearts | 1 Heart |
| 4–6 Hearts | 2 Hearts |
| 7–9 Hearts | 3 Hearts |

3. **Victory:** If you fall off the platform, you lose a heart. The team that eliminates all opposing players first wins.

---

## 🔔 The Doom Bell
The Doom Bell is positioned at the back of each game field, surrounded by a **5-block radius circle** (marked by a pink line).

* **Boundary:** Players **cannot enter** this circle; the bell must be hit from a distance.
* **Function:** If a bell is hit—**even by its own team**—that team loses the round instantly.

---

## 🛠️ Technical & Fair Play Rules

### Disconnections
If a player leaves during a game due to reasons outside their control, their hearts will be transferred to a **placeholder**. This ensures the team doesn't lose its "Total Heart" count.
* If the player rejoins, they take back their hearts.
* If the disconnection lasts too long, the **Host** may decide to **disqualify** the player to keep the game moving.
* A game might be Paused or restarted due to unfaires in Teams due to Players leaving.

### Disqualification
This game is built on **fair play**. Any behavior involving cheats, abuse of game mechanics, or poor sportsmanship can lead to disqualification.

* **Cheats:** Use of external cheat systems or prohibited Minecraft clients is strictly forbidden.
* **Bugs:** All discovered bugs must be reported to an admin. Exploiting bugs for an advantage results in disqualification.
* **Decisions:** All disqualifications are final and decided by the **Hosts**. 

> **Note:** Disqualifications can occur for violating the "spirit of the game," even if a specific rule isn't listed. If you are unsure about a tactic, ask an admin first.
